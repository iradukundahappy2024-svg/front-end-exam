Our Story

From a Small Idea to
a National Movement

Youth Empowerment Rwanda (YER) was born in 2015 from the vision of three young Rwandans who saw a gap: thousands of talented, energetic young people with no structured pathways to develop their potential.

Starting in a single room in Kicukiro with 15 youth participants, YER has grown into a nationally recognized organization serving over 3,200 youth annually across all 30 districts of Rwanda.

Our approach combines practical skills training, mentorship, psychological support, and community-based action — because true empowerment addresses the whole person.

Our Mission

To equip young Rwandans with the skills, knowledge, networks, and confidence to lead productive and meaningful lives and contribute to national development.

Our Vision

A Rwanda where every young person, regardless of background, has equal access to opportunities and the tools to shape their own destiny.

Our Approach

Holistic, community-driven, youth-led programming that combines practical skills with mentorship, psychological support, and civic engagement.

What Drives Us

Our Core Values

Ubuntu

We believe in the African philosophy that "I am because we are." Community, collaboration, and shared growth are at the heart of everything we do.

Innovation

We embrace new ideas, creative solutions, and technology as key tools for unlocking youth potential in the modern economy.

Integrity

We are transparent, accountable, and honest in all that we do — with our participants, partners, donors, and the public.

Inclusivity

We actively work to include youth from all backgrounds — regardless of gender, disability status, economic situation, or geography.

Impact Focus

Everything we do is measured against real outcomes in the lives of young people. We are driven by evidence and results.

Sustainability

We build programs and community systems that outlast our direct involvement, creating lasting change that compounds over generations.

Our Journey

A Decade of Growth

2015

Foundation

YER founded in Kicukiro with 15 youth participants, a single training room, and a big dream to transform Rwanda's youth landscape.

2017

National Recognition

Registered as a national NGO; expanded to 5 districts. Launched the flagship Leadership Academy with support from GIZ Rwanda.

2019

Digital Expansion

Launched the Digital Skills Hub, training 400+ youth in coding and tech entrepreneurship. Partnered with MTN Rwanda Foundation.

2021

COVID Resilience

Pivoted to virtual programming during the pandemic, reaching 800+ youth online. Launched Mental Wellness program in response to rising youth anxiety.

2023

Award & Scale

Won Best NGO Rwanda Award. Expanded to all 30 districts. Launched Mentorship Network with 200+ professional mentors.

2024–2025

Next Chapter

Strategic partnership with Ministry of Youth for national rollout. Target: 10,000 youth reached by end of 2025. East African expansion in planning.
