Frequently Asked Questions

Who can apply to YER programs?

Our programs are open to young Rwandans aged 15–35. Some programs have specific requirements (e.g., the AgriTech program targets youth in rural areas). All programs are free of charge for eligible participants.

How do I apply for a program?

Fill out the contact form on this page selecting "Program Application" as the subject, or visit our Programs page to learn about specific program timelines. We accept rolling applications and cohorts start every quarter.

Are YER programs really free?

Yes, all our programs are fully subsidized through grants, donations, and partner support. Participants are never charged tuition or program fees. Some programs also provide transport stipends for participants from outside Kigali.

How can I become a mentor?

We welcome professionals from all fields to join our Mentorship Network. Fill out the contact form with the subject "Volunteer / Mentor" and tell us about your background and the type of youth you'd like to mentor. Our team will follow up within 3 business days.

How does YER use donations?

82% of every donation goes directly to program delivery and participant support. 10% covers operational overhead, and 8% goes toward program evaluation and learning. We publish annual financial reports on our website for full transparency.
